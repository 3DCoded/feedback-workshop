A Framework: The Core Competencies

The ACGME and ABMS developed the 6 Core Competencies necessary for a practicing physician
  1. Patient Care and Procedural Skills
  2. Medical Knowledge
  3. Practice-based Learning and Improvement
  4. Interpersonal and Communication Skills
  5. Professionalism
  6. Systems-based Practice
Each specialty was tasked with crafting specific milestones within each Competency

"Classroom" Complexity

Learner Vulnerability

  • Don't know what they don't know
  • The "usual" is new or unusual
  • Critical evaluation and summative comments involved

Teacher Vulnerability

  • Monthly public grades by patients & employer
  • Trained to keep concerns private
  • Ultimate accountability and responsibility to patients
  • Long-term continuity & relationship management

Faculty

Learners

  • Rotation goals & objectives

Feedback types

Formal feedback - Most easily recognized type of feedback

  • Structured, often uses a formal evaluation method
  • end-of-rotation narrative
  • Mostly offucs at specified interfals
  • mid-rotation, end-of-rotation
  • More thorough and may last 15-20 minutes or longer

Informal feedback - Informal feedback is not always as obvious

  • Given or asked for in the moment
  • Only takes a few minutes
  • Often specific to a particular skill or patient encounter

Feedback Characteristics

  • Specific
  • Directly observed
  • Timely
  • Relevant to learnign objectives
  • Focused on behavior/skill that can change
  • Not personal

Feedback Barriers

Faculty

  • Time constraints
  • Unclear expectations of competence
  • Uncomfortable giving negative feedback
  • Fear of retribution

Learner

  • Time constraints
  • Unknown expectations
  • Uncomfortable asking certain faculty
  • Uncomfortable adding work

Managing Feedback Barriers

Faculty

  • Set aside time
  • Quite, private space
  • Balance corrective with reinforcing
  • Manage volume and intensity
  • Practice!

Learner

  • Ask for time for feedback
  • Be actively engaged
  • Self-reflect prior
  • Come prepared to grow
  • Come prepared with questions

Managing Difficult Feedback

Faculty

  • Focus your efforts
  • Be transparent
  • Link comments to curriculum
  • Use evaluation tool language-Milestones!!
  • Ask for learner input & insights
  • End with actionable plan

Learner

  • Listen - well-done feedback is meant to be constructive
  • Take a deep breath and think before responding
  • Ask for clarification, examples and suggestions

Feedback tips to influence future behavior

  • Maintain a Positive environment
  • Communicate the Goals of feedback
  • Comment only from your Direct Observation
  • Time sensitive - immediate may not be best
  • Ask the Learner for a Self-Assessment
  • Confirm learner understanding/acceptance
  • Address Emotions
  • Create an Action Plan

Transfer of learning

  • Application of skill or knowledge to new situation
  • Facilitated by cuing or guiding

Mindfulness

  • Systematic, careful attention, a heightened awareness

Reflection

  • Deliberate recall and review of an event
  • Requires active involvement

Cognitive load

  • Amount of information a person is able to absorb, process and retain from any given task
  • Limitations in human cognitive learning processes

Negotiating Goals

  • Fellows need adequate time to engage in productive struggle to advance their skills
  • A trainee can feel like a failure if the supervisor takes over the procedure too quickly
  • Trainer and trainee determine what portion of the procedure is likely to be performed by the trainee and under what circumstances the trainer is likely to take over

Some Tips on Feedback

  • Be constructive and specific
  • Focus on the last thing that the trainee did correctly before any error occurred
  • Then constructive language can be used to pinpoint the problem
  • General praise isn't constructive either
  • No room for improvement
  • Use Facilitative Feedback to Develop Problem Solving Skills
  • Have learner reflect on what happened, analyze their performance, and recognize areas of strength or that need improvement.
